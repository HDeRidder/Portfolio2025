LORA The Explorer

Course: Embedded Devices Essentials Course

Assignment: Developed a weather balloon to establish an emergency network in disaster areas.

Technology:

  • #PCB
  • #ESP32
  • #Networks
  • #LORA

Team: Mathieu Du Jardin, Jens Geenen, and Harold De Ridder

Context & Background

Together with two fellow students, Mathieu Du Jardin and Jens Geenen, I worked on this project for the Embedded Devices Essentials course. We had already received instruction on the ESP32 microcontroller and programming in C. Then we were allowed to get started on our own. We chose the following project:

Project Weatherballoon & Backup Network

"Design a weather balloon with measuring equipment that also serves as a node in a mesh network."

Each balloon had to collect environmental data and contain Wi-Fi and LORA. LORA (LOng RAnge) is a wireless communication technology with a much greater range than Wi-Fi.

After some brainstorming (thanks to ChatGPT), we named the project LORA The Explorer. The aim: to use balloons as an emergency network in disaster areas. Users would connect to the balloon via Wi-Fi, while the balloons would communicate with each other via LORA. This way, an entire village could be bridged with just a few balloons.

Realization

We were enthusiastic and immediately dove into LORA, mesh networks, weather balloons, and PCB design. We had to make our own circuit board and even tried to design the ESP32 module entirely ourselves. We used EasyEDA for this.

We quickly realized how complex it was to build a working microcontroller from scratch. A small mistake could cause everything to fail. Due to time pressure, we replaced our ESP32 chip at the last minute with a version from another manufacturer – a mistake. The PCB looked good, but the chip didn't work. It turned out to be outdated and unprogrammable. After getting help via Facebook (thanks to Roma from Georgia), we managed to get it partially working, but it was too late to successfully complete the project.

What did I apply?

  • Learn to work well with EasyEDA and PCB design.
  • Designing a complex PCB takes a lot of time.
  • First, create a properly functioning test setup before ordering a PCB.
  • Carefully check all components and datasheets.
  • Use forums and online groups when you encounter problems.
  • Respect deadlines when ordering – a lesson we unfortunately learned too late.
