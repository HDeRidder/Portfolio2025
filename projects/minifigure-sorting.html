Minifigure Sorting

Course: Digital Innovation Course / Bricks & More fair

Assignment: Program a robot arm to sort LEGO minifigures into 4 categories using AI.

Technology:

  • #Cobot
  • #RaspberryPi
  • #AI
  • #Python

Team: Harold De Ridder, Robbe Van Hout, Robbe Vangeel, Dylan Van den Broeck

What did I apply?

  • Working with Teachable Machine, a pre-trained AI from Google
  • Programming a Cobot
  • Brushed up on Python knowledge
  • Combining Raspberry Pi and Robotics

Context & Background

For the Digital Innovation course, we were given the assignment...

The assignment:
Make sure children can make little men...

The 4 categories were: ...

Realization

To realize this, we used Teachable Machines. This is a pre-trained AI model from Google that you can use to recognize things. These things can be images, sounds or body positions. We chose images and started taking thousands of pictures of hundreds of different self-made males that each belonged to one category. Based on this input, we taught the AI model to distinguish minifigures from each other.

Next, we got to work on the Cobot. A cobot is a robot designed so that there are virtually no risks involved in using it around humans. If the robot is making a move and bumps into something (even if only gently), it blocks immediately to avoid accidents. We used the wizard on the accompanying tablet to program 4 different scenarios that the robot arm could be presented with. Each scenario was a path that this robotic arm would follow, each time starting with picking up the male, then making a nice pendulum movement to finally put the male down in 1 of the 4 spots.

Finally, we connected a webcam to a Raspberry Pi. The webcam served to take a picture of the created little man. The Raspberry Pi was in turn connected to a relay board that switched inputs of the Cobot. The flow was as follows:

  1. The webcam took a picture that was stored on the Raspberry Pi
  2. The photo was forwarded to an online AI model via the Raspberry Pi
  3. The AI model tried to categorize the photo and forwarded that category to the Raspberry Pi
  4. The raspberry pi switched 1 of 4 relays based on the returned result
  5. The robot executed the corresponding pre-programmed scenario and (hopefully) put the male in the right place
