My Internship

Course: Internship

Assignment:Developed an IoT system to monitor and automate a dust extraction system.

Technology:

  • ESP32 Microcontroller
  • Optocoupler
  • Relay Boards
  • Siemens Contactor
  • Three-color lamp with buzzer

Team: Harold De Ridder and mentor/supervisor from CERcuits

Context & Background

The internship assignment at CERcuits consisted of developing a system that automates the extraction system of the production lines based on laser activity. This Air Filtration Control System (AFCS) automatically switches the blowers on/off and monitors the status of the filter bags. The aim was to reduce energy costs and to go through a learning process in the complete development of a product — from analysis to implementation. Initially, the blowers were running almost continuously, which led to unnecessary energy consumption and faster wear and tear. By improving this situation, approximately 8,400 kWh could be saved annually, representing a reduction in energy costs of €2,772.97. The investment (€711.48 for four systems) was recouped within the first year.

Realization

The development of AFCS included extensive analysis, hardware selection, connection diagrams, and software development. Testing, experimentation, and optimization were carried out to create a professional end product. During this process, the student encountered various technical obstacles, such as incorrectly connected components, interference problems, and damaged parts. Through troubleshooting, help from colleagues, and searching for proper cabling and EMI suppression, the system was built to be robust and reliable. The transition from prototype to professional system required attention to safety, heat management, and finishing.

What did I learn?

  • Code development: The original non-scalable code was rewritten according to the principles of object-oriented programming, making the system expandable.
  • Troubleshooting & assembly: I learned how to detect and resolve errors in cabling and hardware using measuring instruments such as a multimeter.
  • Professional approach: Building a durable and safe hardware solution with EMI protection, signaling, and proper cabling demonstrated the importance of professional finishing.
  • Soft skills: Independence, accepting feedback, learning from experienced colleagues, and setting priorities were strongly emphasized.
  • Hard skills: Improved knowledge of electronics, soldering, Arduino, PlatformIO, PCB production, and scalable software development.
